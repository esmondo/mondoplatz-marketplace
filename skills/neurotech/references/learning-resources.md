## Major Neuroscience Journals

### Top-Tier General Neuroscience
- **Nature Neuroscience** - Highest impact, cutting-edge research
- **Neuron** - Top-tier cell/systems neuroscience
- **Journal of Neuroscience** - Society for Neuroscience flagship journal
- **Annual Review of Neuroscience** - Comprehensive review articles
- **Trends in Neurosciences** - Reviews of current topics

- **JAMA Neurology** - Clinical neurology from JAMA
- **Annals of Neurology** - Clinical and translational research
- **Neurology** - Official journal of American Academy of Neurology

- **Clinical Neurophysiology** - EEG, EMG, evoked potentials
- **NeuroImage** - Brain imaging methods and applications
- **Journal of Neural Engineering** - Neural interfaces and prosthetics
- **Brain-Computer Interfaces** - Dedicated BCI journal

- **Nature Reviews Neuroscience** - Broad reviews
- **Cognitive, Affective, & Behavioral Neuroscience** - Cognition and emotion
- **Cerebral Cortex** - Cortical function and structure

### Neuropsychiatry
- **Biological Psychiatry** - Neurobiology of mental disorders
- **Molecular Psychiatry** - Molecular mechanisms
- **American Journal of Psychiatry** - Clinical psychiatry
- **Neuropsychopharmacology** - Drug effects on brain and behavior

## Learning Pathways

### For Different Goals

**Beginner (General Neurotech Literacy)**:
1. Start: Khan Academy nervous system videos
2. Read: Neuroscience News articles regularly
3. Book: "Neuroscience: Exploring the Brain" (intro chapters)
4. Course: Coursera "Understanding the Brain"
5. Join: BCI Society (free membership)

**Intermediate (Technical Skills)**:
1. Course: MIT OCW 9.01 or Coursera Computational Neuroscience
2. Learn Python: MNE tutorials
3. Book: "An Introduction to the Event-Related Potential Technique"
4. Practice: Download open datasets, replicate analyses
5. Conference: Attend BCI or SfN (poster sessions)

**Advanced (Research/Development)**:
1. Deep dive: Specialized courses in your area
2. Read: Primary literature extensively (10+ papers/week)
3. Network: Attend conferences, present work
4. Collaborate: Reach out to researchers
5. Contribute: Open source projects, publish findings

**Business/Market Focus**:
1. Industry: NeurotechJP, IEEE Brain news
2. Reports: CB Insights, market research
3. Network: LinkedIn, industry conferences
4. Understand: Read Evidence Book, Guidebook
5. Connect: Attend startup/business events in neurotech

## Evaluation & Critical Thinking

When consuming any resource:
- Check credentials of authors/sources
- Look for conflicts of interest
- Compare multiple sources
- Question extraordinary claims
- Seek primary sources
- Consider recency of information

For scientific papers, always check:
- Sample size adequate?
- Control groups present?
- Methods clearly described?
- Results match conclusions?
- Limitations acknowledged?
- Conflicts of interest disclosed?

See `evidence-based-validation.md` for detailed framework.
